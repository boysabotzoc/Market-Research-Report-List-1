<p><p>The Torque Vectoring Differential market is segmented into Active and Passive types. Active Torque Vectoring Differentials use real-time data to adjust torque distribution between wheels for improved handling and stability, enhancing performance in various driving conditions. Conversely, Passive Torque Vectoring Differentials offer a more mechanical approach, utilizing simple gear designs and limited-slip capabilities to distribute torque based on wheel slip, providing reliable performance without electronic intervention. Both types aim to optimize traction and control in vehicles.</p></p>
