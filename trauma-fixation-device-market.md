<p><p>The trauma fixation device market comprises various types, categorized mainly into internal and external fixation. Internal fixation devices include plates, screws, nails, and other mechanisms used to stabilize fractures internally. Plates are flat devices affixed to bones, screws secure bone fragments together, and nails are elongated devices inserted into bone shafts. External fixation involves stabilizing fractures from outside the body using external frames and pins. These devices are crucial for effective fracture healing and restoring limb functionality.</p></p>
